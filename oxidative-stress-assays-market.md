<p><p>Oxidative stress assays encompass various market types that assess cellular oxidative damage or antioxidant defenses. Indirect assays measure markers of oxidative damage, while antioxidant capacity assays evaluate an organism's ability to neutralize free radicals. Enzyme-based assays focus on specific enzymes that mitigate oxidative stress, highlighting their roles in biochemical pathways. Reactive oxygen species (ROS)-based assays directly detect and quantify ROS levels, providing insight into oxidative stress status. Collectively, these assays are vital for research in aging, cancer, and neurodegenerative diseases.</p></p>
