<p><p>The Non-Agriculture Intelligent Irrigation Controller market features two primary types: weather-based controllers and sensor-based controllers. Weather-based controllers utilize real-time weather data to optimize irrigation schedules, reducing water usage by adjusting to conditions like rainfall and temperature. In contrast, sensor-based controllers rely on ground or atmospheric sensors to assess soil moisture levels, ensuring water is applied only when necessary. Both types aim to enhance water efficiency and promote sustainable landscaping practices in non-agricultural settings.</p></p>
