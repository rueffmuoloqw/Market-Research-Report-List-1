<p><p>The compostable plastics market includes several key types. Polylactic Acid (PLA) is derived from renewable resources like corn starch and is used in packaging and disposable items. Polyhydroxy Fatty Acid (PHA) is produced by bacteria from organic materials, offering biodegradability. Thermoplastic starch combines starch with other materials for flexible applications. Polybutylene Succinate (PBS) is a versatile, biodegradable alternative for various uses. Additionally, "Others" encompasses emerging compostable materials and blends that cater to specific applications and consumer needs.</p></p>
