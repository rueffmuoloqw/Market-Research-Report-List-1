<p><p>The buffered fiber optics market is divided into two main types: indoor fiber optics and outdoor fiber optics. Indoor fiber optics are designed for internal installations, providing reliable connectivity within buildings, often featuring enhanced cable flexibility and protection against physical stress. In contrast, outdoor fiber optics are built to withstand harsher environmental conditions, such as moisture, temperature variations, and UV exposure, making them suitable for external installations, including telecommunications networks, utilities, and data centers. Both types cater to specific connectivity needs.</p></p>
